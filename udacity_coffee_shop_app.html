Udacity Coffee Shop App Case Study

This case study was part of my UX design learning process through the Udacity Nanodegree design program. The problem statement was given at the end of the 3-month course and took 1 week to complete.

  • Deliverables: Wireframes, 13 UI Screens and Digital Prototype
  • Project Duration: 1 week

Problem Statement

Udacity is considering opening a digitally-enabled coffee shop experience. As a part of this experience, students will be able to use an app, which exists separately from the Udacity classroom to perform actions such as:

  • Placing their drink orders
  • Collaborating with fellow classmates and students
  • Facilitating a productive study environment

Design Process

For the secondary, I started by carrying out a competitive analysis to understand market needs and opportunities. Next, I went with conducting a public survey with 70+ responses.

As a part of primary research, I conducted semi-structured user interviews with 3 participants in online mode via zoom.

SWOT Analysis

Screenshots from Starbucks App

To gather information and insights about the existing market and opportunities. I considered conducting a SWOT analysis for Starbucks and strategising accordingly.

Strengths

  • Strong loyalty program
  • Reputed brand image
  • Packaging
  • Quality and Taste

Weakness

  • Confusing user flow to order drinks
  • Account creation
  • Crash issues
  • Intimidating error

Opportunity

  • Geographical expansion
  • Online payment
  • Cloud cafes
  • Bean subscription

Threat

  • Third-party delivery
  • Expensive coffee beans
  • COVID restrictions

Public Surveys

I kept the survey question list very short to not move away from the scope of the problem and gather only relevant responses. Here are the questions:

  • What time of day do you usually drink coffee?
  • Where do you usually consume your drink?
  • Type of coffee preference?
  • How often do you consume coffee?
  • Which outlet do you prefer for your coffee?

Insights Drawn

  • 77% of people drink coffee before their first meal of the day.
  • 21% of people are completely dependent on cafes to get their coffee.
  • 84% of people like their coffee with some additives, rest like black coffee.
  • 89% of people drink coffee on regular basis.
  • 41% of the people prefer Starbucks, 12% prefer Jab, 8% prefer Dunkin and 39% to other outlets.

User Interviews

As mentioned, there were 3 participants over a zoom call whom I interviewed and asked a few probing questions as well to dig deep into their behavioural reasonings. Here are some questions from the questionnaire:

  • What's your eating regime like in a day?
  • Which is your favourite cafe? followed by Why?
  • When was the last time you visited a cafe?
  • How did you choose what to drink?
  • How did you order your drink?
  • What kind of instructions did you give while ordering?
  • What do you prefer between going to a cafe and ordering drinks online?

Insights Drawn

  • Coffee is a major part of daily lifestyle
  • People choose cafes firstly depending upon the ambience and then the quality of the drink.
  • People choose their favourite drink or either order something which was suggested to them or something which is famous for a brewery when confused.
  • Personalisation with their drink is something which people value a lot.
  • People go to cafes to meet their friends and work in a vibrant environment.
  • Discounts or freebies are rare but they are exciting when given.
  • They get confused with the menu without drink description
  • Crowded cafes make the experience unpleasant.
